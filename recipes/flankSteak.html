Flank Steak

Flank steak is a flavorful and versatile cut of beef that comes from the lower belly of the cow. It is a lean and fibrous cut of meat that is best when cooked quickly at high heat.

Flank steak is often marinated to help tenderize the meat and enhance its flavor, and can be grilled, broiled, or seared in a pan. It is commonly used in dishes such as fajitas, stir-fry, and salads, and can also be served as a main dish alongside vegetables and starches. Flank steak is a popular choice for those looking for a tasty and nutritious meal that is easy to prepare.

Ingredients

  • ½ cup vegetable oil
  • ⅓ cup low-sodium soy sauce
  • ¼ cup red wine vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
  • 1 ½ tablespoons Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • ½ teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1 (1 1/2-pound) flank steak

Steps

  1. Gather all ingredients.
  2. Overhead shot of measured ingredients gathered for marinated flank steak
  3. Whisk together oil, soy sauce, vinegar, lemon juice, Worcestershire sauce, Dijon mustard, garlic, and pepper for marinade until thoroughly combined. Place steak in a 9x13-inch glass baking dish.
  4. Overhead view of a bowl of marinade next to a baking dish with flank steak
  5. Pour marinade over flank steak in the baking dish; turn several times to coat thoroughly with marinade. Cover, and refrigerate for 2 to 6 hours, or up to 12 hours if you have time.
  6. Overhead view of flank steak in a baking dish being marinated
  7. When ready to cook, preheat an outdoor grill for medium-high heat and lightly oil the grate.
  8. Remove steak from the marinade and shake off excess. Discard the remaining marinade.
  9. Cook steak on the preheated grill for about 5 minutes per side, or to desired doneness.
  10. Overhead closeup of flank steak being cooked on a charcoal grill
  11. Remove from the grill and let rest for 5 minutes before slicing and serving.
  12. Serve hot and enjoy!
